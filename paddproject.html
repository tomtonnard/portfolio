Drugwaste
Instruction App

research - usability testing/prototyping -
UX/UI design

This project was realised in collaboration with the Province of Limburg,
the Police and Fire Department.

I've developed a high fidelity mobile web-application that informs the user about drugwaste dumps, makes registrating easier and shows the locations on a map.

Discovery
Phase

Within the province of Limburg, there is an unacceptable number of illegal drug waste dumpings. Considering the consequences for the environment and public health, as well as the often high cleanup costs, there is a need from the government to handle drug waste dumpings in a uniform manner.

A user-friendly application is needed to request information about drug waste, register the site quickly and to get a better overview of the region it's been dumped in.

1.

The big question

How do I create a user-friendly application that informs the user about drugwaste, shows the locations on a map and makes it possible to fill in the registration form digitally?

2.

Interviews

Various parties have been interviewed about their interactions with drugwaste. What was important is the cases where the interaction with drugwaste went wrong.

Also the police noted that the filling in of the registration form was still done by paper and took too much time.

3.

Ideation

Knowing the user is the most important before any ideation can start. Personas were created to have a clear visual of the user of the app.

Paper First! With the info aquired by the interviews, sketching and wireframing commenced to establish the main functionalities of the app.

4.

Prototyping the product

Prototypes were created and tested with the users from various target groups. Multiple rounds led to multiple iterations and refinement.

Evolution

Countless rounds of prototypes, user testing, and tech exploration helped to refine the finalised interface and product.

The following screenshots are low fidelity prototype sketches slowly becoming more concrete with design and colors implemented.

Finished Product

After rounds of testing, a redesign of the information section was realised, as well as a more minimalistic design.
